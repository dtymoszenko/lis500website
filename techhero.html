Tech Hero #1: Anu Bharadwaj

Inspired by her dedication to diversity and positive change in business and technology, we choose Anu Bharadwaj as our tech hero. Anu is an industry professional with more than 20 years in executive leadership, her passion for driving social change and promoting diversity led her to her current role as Director of Atlassian Foundation. This foundation's mission is to unleash the potential of social impact teams by developing a new approach to corporate philanthropy that focuses on changing lives through education. In addition to her work at the Atlassian Foundation, Anu feeds her passion for inspiring diverse leaders by utilizing her position as a member of the Operator Collective venture to empower diverse founders.


Anu graduated with a Bachelor’s in Computer Engineering from RV College Of Engineering. She began her illustrious career as a software developer for Microsoft. At Microsoft, she led a team that was responsible for enhancing Microsoft’s visual studio. After ten years at Microsoft, Anu took the job as the Head of Product for Jira, a bug/ issue tracking software developed by Atlassian.


Anu’s climb to the top at Atlassian was quick and steady. She moved from Head of Product for Atlassian Cloud, to Chief Operating Officer, and now President, introducing several services such as Atlassian Home and People Directory along the way. Her affinity for leadership and ambitious personality shine in her successful and exciting tech career. While under her presidentship, Atlassian has added more than $4 billion to its market cap.


Anu’s immense success and dedication to the success of other diverse leaders is inspiring. In 2016 during the prime of her career at Atlassian, Anu took a sabbatical to do field work in wildlife conservation projects. She spent time monitoring penguin rookeries in Antarctica and putting GPS collars on cheetahs in Namibia. Truly a special and inspirational tech leader, Anu Bharadwaj’s contributions to the rise of diverse leadership in the tech industry is remarkable. By using her voice to advocate for diversity and inclusion, Anu is creating a world where diverse tech leaders and entrepreneurs are encouraged to thrive.

Tech Hero #2: Cadran Cowansage

Cadran Cowansage is a brilliant software engineer who is responsible for the creation of Elpha, a professional, venture backed network of high-achieving women in tech. Before taking on this endeavor, Cardan had a successful career in the tech industry. She earned a Bachelor's Degree from Colby college, majoring in computer science and biology. She then went on to earn her Master's Degree in Biomedical Informatics from Columbia. She began her tech career in 2012, working as a platform manager for Flatiron Health in New York City. After working at Flatiron for 5 months, Cadran went to work as a software engineer at MongoDB. She stayed at this position for just over two years before accepting a position as a tech lead for Y Combinator.


Cadran stayed with Y Combinator for three years before starting Elpha. At Elpha she served as the founder and CEO. Elpha was an extremely successful venture. At its peak, the company had more than 100,000 members. Elpha was not your regular community. Its members found in it a truly accepting space where they could share their experiences in the industry and expect a plethora of professional support and advice from the extensive community of women in the tech industry. Cadran stepped down as the CEO of Elpha in January of 2025. She has also worked as a member of the technical staff at Open AI since 2023.


In addition to her work at Elpha, which was based on professional communication, Cadran is responsible for starting another online space for hybrid professional and social communication where the core culture and the product and software decisions were all set by women. This product is called Leap. Leap is a space for civil discourse and authentic conversations where users interact using their real identities. Cadran’s intent with Leap is for this discourse to help women in tech connect to other women in the tech space and through conversations and personal connections help propel women within the industry. Cadran started this social network after realizing that there was a lack of representation and connection between all of the smart and powerful women she knew who worked in tech. Using her extensive technical and social skills, Cadran has created both social and professional platforms providing women with connection, hope, and prosperity within the tech industry. She truly is a tech hero.
