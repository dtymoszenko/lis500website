Implicit Bias Definition

Implicit bias impacts how we perceive other people in ways we don’t consciously realize, often reinforcing harmful stereotypes and hurtful rhetoric. Implicit association tests (IAT”s) were developed in the late 1990’s and have been used ever since to increase people's awareness of the unconscious impact their implicit biases have on their perceptions of different groups of people. Intersectionality expands on this awareness by highlighting the ways in which people's overlapping identities intersect to create unique instances of social oppression or advantage.
